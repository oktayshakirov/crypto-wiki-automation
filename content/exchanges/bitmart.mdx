## BitMart: A Global Hub for Altcoin Access and Retail-Friendly Trading

Founded in 2017 by Sheldon Xia, BitMart is a globally oriented cryptocurrency exchange known for listing emerging tokens early and offering a retail-friendly app across 180+ jurisdictions. Headquartered operationally in Asia with international offices, it targets users who want quick access to trending digital assets alongside straightforward spot and futures markets. On BitMart, users can trade major assets like **[Bitcoin](/posts/what-is-bitcoin)** and **[Ethereum](/posts/what-is-ethereum)** as well as a broad catalog of **[altcoins](/posts/what-are-altcoins)**.

## Core Services and Ecosystem

BitMart’s platform spans spot markets, derivatives, and earning products, while emphasizing simplicity for everyday users. Below are the core pillars most traders interact with:

- **Trading Platform:**  
  - **Spot Trading:** Competitive maker/taker fees with tiered discounts, advanced charting, and dozens of order types beyond simple market/limit (e.g., post-only, IOC). BitMart is particularly active in listing small- and mid-cap tokens, which appeals to traders who study **[blockchain](/posts/what-is-blockchain)** projects beyond large caps.  
  - **Futures (Perpetuals):** Linear and inverse perpetual swaps on major pairs, typically supporting up to triple-digit leverage in eligible regions. While leverage can amplify gains, it increases loss potential and margin-call risk; newer traders often start with smaller position sizes and no isolated-to-cross leverage jumps.  
  - **Margin Trading:** Available on selected pairs with cross/isolated modes. BitMart’s margin module highlights risk metrics (maintenance margin, liquidation price) prominently to help users manage exposure.  
  - **Liquidity and Pairs:** BitMart is best known for its breadth of listings. Altcoin exposure tends to be higher than on mainstream US venues. For US-style compliance and fewer listings, traders sometimes compare BitMart with **[Coinbase](/exchanges/coinbase)**; for deeper altcoin variety, a common peer is **[KuCoin](/exchanges/kucoin)**.

- **Earning and On-Exchange Yield:**  
  - **Flexible Savings and Promotions:** Periodic promotional rates on specific tokens, with flexible or fixed terms. APYs fluctuate, and promotional caps apply.  
  - **Staking:** On-chain and pseudo-staking features for a rotating roster of assets, generally focused on **[Proof-of-Stake](/posts/what-is-proof-of-stake)** networks. Yield varies by network conditions; it can complement a longer-term portfolio. If you’re new to yield-generating assets, see our primer on **[staking](/posts/crypto-staking)**.  
  - **Auto-Invest/DCA:** Dollar-cost averaging plans into recurring purchases on selected spot pairs, helpful for smoothing entries in volatile markets.

- **Launch and Listings:**  
  - **Launchpad/Launchpool Programs:** Periodic token sales, airdrop-style pools, and early-access distributions for vetted projects. These opportunities are speculative; users should apply strict **DYOR** and consider diversification.  
  - **New Listings:** BitMart frequently adds tokens from trending sectors (AI, RWA, GameFi), providing faster exposure than many mainstream exchanges. Staying informed about **[DeFi](/posts/what-is-defi)** narratives helps users evaluate which new tokens align with their strategy.

- **Payments, Transfers, and Fiat Access:**  
  - **Fiat Channels:** Third-party payment processors for card purchases of crypto, plus P2P marketplace options in select regions. Pricing and KYC verification depend on the provider and jurisdiction.  
  - **Withdrawals/Deposits:** Multi-chain support enables bridging between networks for lower fees. Users often keep long-term holdings off-exchange; read how to secure assets with **[how to store crypto](/posts/how-to-store-crypto)**.

- **APIs and Tools:**  
  - **API Trading:** REST and WebSocket APIs for market data and order execution.  
  - **Automation:** Grid and trigger-based orders are supported on selected pairs. Experienced traders often supplement discretionary decisions with sentiment tools like the **[Fear and Greed Index](/tools/fear-and-greed-index)**.

## Security Measures

Security is a layered process at BitMart, combining user-account protections with exchange-level custody controls. The exchange’s historical record and ongoing measures include:

- **Account Security:**  
  - **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Google Authenticator or SMS 2FA for logins, withdrawals, and API actions.  
  - **Withdrawal Controls:** Address whitelisting, anti-phishing codes in emails, and device management help reduce phishing or social-engineering risks.  
  - **Session Monitoring:** Device/IP logs and session revocation options let users react quickly if credentials are compromised.

- **Custody and Wallet Infrastructure:**  
  - **Cold/Hot Segregation:** The majority of assets are stored offline in cold wallets; only operational balances remain in hot wallets to facilitate withdrawals. BitMart does not publicly disclose an exact cold-storage percentage, which some users may weigh when choosing venues.  
  - **Risk Monitoring:** Internal monitoring flags anomalous flows. Listing reviews and wallet tagging help mitigate exposure to tainted funds.

- **Incident History and Response:**  
  - **December 2021 Hot Wallet Incident:** BitMart experienced a hot wallet compromise (publicly estimated near $150–200M across multiple networks). The exchange stated it reimbursed affected users with corporate funds and subsequently tightened operational security.  
  - **Transparency and Post-Mortem:** While communication following the incident included reimbursements and security updates, many risk-conscious users still keep the majority of long-term funds in self-custody. For practical guidance, understand common pitfalls with **[crypto scams](/posts/how-to-avoid-crypto-scams)** and revisit fundamentals on **[how to store crypto](/posts/how-to-store-crypto)**.

- **Proof-of-Reserves (PoR):**  
  - As of writing, BitMart has not established a routine, auditor-attested, and liabilities-matched PoR disclosure on a set schedule. Some snapshots and wallet pages may appear over time, but users who prioritize independent solvency verification may prefer exchanges that publish frequent, third-party-audited PoR reports.
